My Unexpected Path to CS

Third Year Student at University of Gondar | Still Figuring It Out

How I Stumbled Into Programming

My first encounter with programming was accidental. In 7th grade, I overheard classmates discussing a C++ course. It sounded like something smart people did, and I was intrigued.

"I remember being equal parts intimidated and fascinated when I saw those curly braces and semicolons for the first time. It looked like a secret language, and part of me wanted to understand it."

The Cyber Security Fascination

My fascination with cyber security started when I watched movies featuring ethical hackers — people who used their skills to outsmart systems and protect others. It wasn't just about breaking in; it was about understanding how things worked at a deep level. I started reading about real-world hacks, exploring how digital vulnerabilities were exploited, and how security experts countered them. The more I learned, the more I wanted to be one of them. I also love other it fiedls like software more on game developmet,machine learning,and data science. I am willing to exlploer more about them in the future.

“Cyber security isn't just coding. It's like playing detective in a digital world — you find clues, identify weaknesses, and build stronger systems.”

Where I Am Now

Today, as a third-year Computer Science student at the University of Gondar, I've built projects, debugged errors, and collaborated with classmates on assignments that once felt impossible. I'm still learning every day — whether it's Java, C++, or HTML/CSS — but I now see every challenge as an opportunity to grow.

My focus is expanding beyond just writing code. I'm diving deeper into cyber security fundamentals, from learning how to spot vulnerabilities to practicing secure coding techniques. I also enjoy understanding how front-end design can enhance user trust — which is critical in building secure systems.

Real Talk

I'm not a genius. I'm a learner. I keep going even when things don't work the first time. And that, I’ve found, is more powerful than talent.

What Keeps Me Going

  • That magical moment when code finally works
  • Learning something new, even if it's confusing at first
  • Knowing that even experts were once beginners
  • Dreaming of helping others through tech
